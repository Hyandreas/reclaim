## What Good SaaS Pages Have In Common

- One unmistakable first-screen idea. Linear, Vercel, and Attio do not make the viewer parse six competing regions.
- Product proof is specific. Linear shows an actual issue workflow; Retool shows a prompt-to-app surface; Attio shows a CRM frame.
- Navigation is quiet. The nav exists, but it does not compete with the hero or product proof.
- Color has a job. Stripe uses color as the memorable brand gesture; Linear and Vercel use near-monochrome control; Retool uses atmosphere around one input.
- The best pages use fewer boxes than our current app. They rely on scale, alignment, depth, and strong objects instead of repeated cards.
- Trust appears below or around the main object. Logo strips work when they support the story instead of filling dead space.

## Implications For Reclaim

- Treat the app as a product object, not a dashboard collage. The strongest candidate is a Linear/Retool hybrid: dark case ledger, central evidence object, memo packet as the right rail.
- The first screen should show a compelling selected claim, not a neutral empty state. The user should immediately understand the recovery amount, evidence path, and why the agent is safe.
- Reduce metadata pills. Keep only live backend state, recovered exposure, and primary action visible in the top bar.
- Make the evidence rail look like a timeline receipt, not a stack of cards. The visual metaphor should be audit trail plus source packet.
- Move hackathon/Vultr proof into a compact deploy/status strip or footer, not across the top as a wall of labels.
- If we add a landing/demo intro, Stripe and Vercel are better references than product dashboards. If we polish the app itself, Linear and Retool are better references.
